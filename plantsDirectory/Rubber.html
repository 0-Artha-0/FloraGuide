Rubber Plant (Ficus elastica)

The Rubber Plant (Ficus elastica) is a popular houseplant known for its large, glossy, and leathery leaves, typically deep green or burgundy. Native to Southeast Asia, it’s part of the fig family and can grow several feet tall indoors with the right care. Rubber Plants are hardy and can adapt to various indoor conditions, making them suitable for beginners. They’re also appreciated for their air-purifying qualities, helping to remove toxins from the air. With proper care, this resilient plant can make a bold, elegant statement in any home.

Watering

Rubber plants prefer consistently moist soil but do not like to be waterlogged. They can tolerate slight drying out between waterings but should not be left dry for extended periods.

Frequency

Water every 1–2 weeks, allowing the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings. In winter, reduce watering frequency as the plant enters a dormant phase and requires less moisture.

Technique

Water thoroughly until you see water draining from the pot’s drainage holes. Empty the saucer after watering to prevent the plant from sitting in water, which can lead to root rot.

Soil

Rubber Plants thrive in a well-draining potting mix. A combination of peat, pine bark, and a bit of sand or perlite works well, as this provides both moisture retention and good drainage. The plant doesn’t do well in compacted soil, so a light, aerated mix is essential to prevent root rot.

Light

Prefers bright, indirect light for optimal growth. While it can adapt to low light, the plant may grow slower and develop smaller leaves. Direct sunlight can scorch the leaves, so avoid placing it in harsh, direct sun. If grown in low light, try rotating it occasionally to ensure even growth.

Temperature

Prefers temperatures between 60–75°F (16–24°C). It doesn’t tolerate cold drafts, so avoid placing it near windows, doors, or air conditioning vents that might expose it to sudden temperature drops.

Fertilization

Feed with a balanced liquid fertilizer every month during the growing season (spring and summer). Avoid fertilizing in fall and winter when the plant’s growth slows down, as this can lead to nutrient buildup in the soil

Pruning

Prune in spring or early summer to control the plant’s shape and encourage branching. Remove any leggy or weak growth, cutting just above a node to promote fuller, bushier growth. Always use clean, sharp scissors or pruning shears.

Humidity

Rubber Plants prefer moderate humidity. Misting the leaves occasionally can help increase humidity, especially in dry environments. Alternatively, placing a humidifier nearby or using a humidity tray can create a more favorable atmosphere.

Air Circulation

Good airflow helps prevent fungal issues and keeps the plant healthy. Avoid placing it in closed, stagnant areas. While Rubber Plants don’t need a breeze, some air circulation is beneficial to discourage pests.

Pest Management

Watch for common pests like spider mites, scale, and mealybugs, which can affect Rubber Plants. If pests are present, wipe the leaves with a damp cloth and use insecticidal soap or neem oil as needed. Regularly inspecting the plant will help catch issues early.

Repotting

Repot every 2–3 years, or when the plant becomes root-bound, to provide it with fresh soil and room to grow. Use a pot one size up from the current pot, and avoid pots that are too large, as excess soil can lead to water retention issues.
