Pothos Plant (Epipremnum aureum)

Pothos (Epipremnum aureum) is a popular, low-maintenance houseplant known for its heart-shaped leaves and trailing vines. It thrives in a variety of light conditions, from low light to bright, indirect light, and is perfect for beginners due to its adaptability. Pothos can tolerate irregular watering, preferring to dry out slightly between waterings.

Watering

Pothos prefer to dry out a bit between waterings but do not tolerate prolonged dryness. The soil should be kept slightly moist but not soggy.

Frequency

Water when the top 1–2 inches of soil feel dry to the touch. Typically, this is every 1–2 weeks, depending on the season and the plant’s environment.

Technique

Water thoroughly at the base of the plant, ensuring that water drains out of the bottom of the pot. Avoid watering from the top of the leaves, as this can promote fungal growth or water spots on the leaves.

Soil

Pothos prefer well-draining, all-purpose potting mix. A standard potting mix works well, but adding perlite, orchid bark, or sand can improve drainage and prevent the roots from becoming waterlogged.

Light

Pothos thrive in bright, indirect light, which enhances their growth rate and leaf color. Placing the plant near a window with sheer curtains or in a spot with filtered light is ideal.

Temperature

Temperature Range: Pothos grow best in temperatures between 60–85°F (16–29°C). They are not frost-tolerant and will struggle in cold temperatures.

Fertilization

During the spring and summer, Pothos actively grow and can be fertilized once a month with a diluted, balanced liquid fertilizer (e.g., 10-10-10). This provides essential nutrients for the plant to grow strong, vibrant leaves.

Pruning

Regular pruning encourages the plant to grow more densely, creating a bushier appearance. Trim leggy stems and cut back vines to the desired length to prevent the plant from becoming too unruly.

Humidity

Pothos do best in moderate to high humidity, but they are adaptable to average indoor conditions. They can tolerate lower humidity but will grow more slowly, and the leaves may not develop to their full size or vibrancy.

Air Circulation

Pothos prefer moderate air circulation to keep the leaves healthy and dry. Good airflow helps prevent fungal issues and keeps the foliage from becoming too damp, which can attract pests.

Pest Management

Pothos can occasionally fall prey to pests such as aphids, spider mites, and mealybugs. These pests often appear on the undersides of leaves and along the stems.

Repotting

Repot Pothos every 1–2 years, or when the plant outgrows its pot or becomes root-bound. Repotting can also be done when you notice the roots growing out of the drainage holes or when the soil begins to lose its structure and drainage capacity.
