Chinese Evergreen (Agloanema)

The Chinese Evergreen, scientifically known as Aglaonema, is a hardy and visually striking houseplant known for its vibrant leaves with splashes of green, silver, red, or pink. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, this plant is admired for its low maintenance and air-purifying abilities.

Fun fact: Chinese Evergreens are considered good luck in Asia, symbolizing long life and prosperity!

Watering

Chinese Evergreens prefer moderately moist soil. Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings but avoid letting it dry out completely. Overwatering can lead to root rot, so moderation is key.

Frequency

Water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch, typically every 7–10 days. During cooler months, reduce watering frequency as growth slows down.

Technique

Water at the soil level to avoid wetting the leaves, which can lead to fungal issues. Water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom, and empty any excess water from the saucer to prevent waterlogging.

Soil

Chinese Evergreens thrive in a well-draining potting mix, ideally a blend of potting soil, perlite, and peat moss. This mix retains moisture without compacting, allowing roots to breathe.

Light

These plants tolerate low to medium light, making them ideal for indoor spaces with limited sunlight. Bright, indirect light enhances their leaf coloration, but avoid direct sun, which can scorch the leaves.

Temperature

Chinese Evergreens prefer temperatures between 65°F and 80°F (18°C to 27°C). Avoid exposure to temperatures below 60°F (15°C), as cold drafts can damage the foliage.

Fertilization

Feed with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer once a month during the growing season (spring and summer). Dilute to half the recommended strength to prevent fertilizer burn.

Pruning

Pruning is minimal with Chinese Evergreens. Simply trim off any yellowing or damaged leaves to encourage fresh growth. Use clean, sharp scissors or pruning shears for a clean cut.

Humidity

Chinese Evergreens enjoy moderate humidity levels. If your home is dry, consider misting the leaves occasionally or using a humidity tray, especially during winter months when indoor air can be dry.

Air Circulation

While not demanding in terms of air circulation, it’s best to avoid overly stagnant areas. Keep away from direct drafts, which can cause leaf damage.

Pest Management

Chinese Evergreens are generally resistant to pests, but occasionally spider mites, aphids, or scale can appear. Wipe the leaves regularly with a damp cloth to keep them clean and deter pests. If needed, treat infestations with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Repotting

Repot every 2–3 years or when the plant becomes root-bound. Choose a pot 1–2 inches larger than the current one, and refresh the soil to support healthy growth.
