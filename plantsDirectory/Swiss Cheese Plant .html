Swiss Cheese Plant (Monstera adansonii)

The Swiss Cheese Plant, also known as Monstera adansonii, is recognized for its unique, hole-filled leaves. This vine plant brings a tropical look and is perfect for hanging baskets or climbing on a trellis.

Watering

The Swiss Cheese Plant prefers to be watered when the top 1–2 inches of soil feel dry. Be cautious with overwatering, as this can lead to root rot. Ensure the pot has proper drainage, and allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings. They can tolerate a bit of neglect but prefer consistent moisture.

Frequency

Water weekly, or when the top inch of soil is dry. Adjust frequency based on season and humidity levels.

Technique

Water thoroughly until water drains out the bottom, ensuring even hydration without soaking the soil.

Soil

Use a rich, well-draining potting mix. A blend of peat and perlite or orchid bark works well for drainage.

Light

Prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate lower light conditions. Avoid direct sunlight to prevent leaf burn.

Temperature

Best temperature range is 65-85°F (18-29°C). Avoid temperatures below 50°F (10°C).

Fertilization

Fertilize every 2-4 weeks in the growing season with a balanced, diluted liquid fertilizer.

Pruning

Prune occasionally for shape and to remove dead leaves. Pruning encourages new, healthy growth.

Humidity

High humidity is preferred. Mist regularly or place near a humidifier for optimal conditions..

Air Circulation

Moderate circulation is ideal to prevent root rot. Avoid placing in enclosed areas.

Pest Management

Check for spider mites and aphids. Treat with insecticidal soap or neem oil if pests appear.

Repotting

Repot every 1-2 years or when rootbound. Use a slightly larger pot to allow growth without oversaturation.
