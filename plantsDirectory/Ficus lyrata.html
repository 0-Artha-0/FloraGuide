Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata)

The Fiddle Leaf Fig is a statement plant admired for its large, violin-shaped leaves and lush, tropical look. It has become an icon in interior design due to its striking appearance, but it does require a bit more care than other houseplants.

Watering

Fiddle Leaf Figs prefer to be kept on the drier side. Water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. Ensure the pot has proper drainage to prevent root rot. Overwatering can lead to yellowing leaves and mold growth. In general, these plants like regular but moderate watering.

Frequency

Water every 1-2 weeks, allowing the soil to dry slightly between waterings. Always check the soil first, as overwatering is one of the most common issues.

Technique

Water the soil thoroughly until water drains from the bottom, but make sure no water sits in the saucer. A deep watering ensures the roots are hydrated without causing soggy soil.

Soil

A well-draining potting mix with added perlite or sand is ideal. Fiddle Leaf Figs are sensitive to root rot, so ensuring good drainage is key.

Light

Prefers bright, indirect light. Place it near a window with filtered sunlight or in a well-lit room. Avoid prolonged direct sunlight, which can scorch the leaves.

Temperature

Optimal temperature is 60-75°F (16-24°C). Avoid cold drafts and sudden temperature changes, which can cause leaf drop.

Fertilization

Feed every 4-6 weeks during spring and summer with a balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half strength. Fertilize less in fall and winter when growth slows down.

Pruning

Prune for shape control or to remove damaged leaves. Fiddle Leaf Figs can grow tall, so occasional pruning keeps them manageable.

Humidity

This plant prefers moderate to high humidity. Mist the leaves occasionally or place a humidifier nearby, especially in dry climates.

Air Circulation

Good airflow is important to prevent mold and pests. Avoid placing it in cramped spaces with stagnant air.

Pest Management

Common pests include scale, spider mites, and mealybugs. Wipe the leaves with a damp cloth regularly to keep them dust-free and check for pests. Treat infestations with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Repotting

Repot every 1-2 years, or when the roots are visibly filling the pot. Choose a pot only slightly larger than the current one to avoid root rot.
