Philodendron (Philodendron hederaceum)

Philodendron is a popular and easy-to-care-for houseplant known for its attractive, heart-shaped or deeply lobed leaves. It is part of the Araceae family and thrives in moderate to bright, indirect light but can tolerate lower light conditions.

Watering

Philodendrons prefer consistently moist soil but do not like to be waterlogged. They can tolerate brief periods of dryness, but letting the soil dry out too much over time can cause stress to the plant.

Frequency

Water the plant when the top 1–2 inches of soil feels dry. The exact frequency will vary depending on temperature, humidity, and the season. Typically, during the growing season (spring and summer), you’ll need to water every 7–10 days.

Technique

Water the plant thoroughly until excess water begins to drain from the bottom of the pot. This ensures the entire root system is hydrated. Always empty the saucer of any standing water to prevent root rot.

Soil

Philodendrons prefer well-draining, lightweight potting mix. A standard potting soil blend with added perlite or orchid bark ensures adequate drainage, preventing root rot, which is a common issue when soil stays too wet.

Light

These plants thrive in bright, indirect light, making them perfect for east or west-facing windows. Direct sunlight can scorch the leaves, particularly the softer, thinner varieties.

Temperature

Philodendrons thrive in temperatures between 65–80°F (18–27°C). They are quite sensitive to temperature fluctuations, so it’s best to avoid sudden temperature changes or placing them in cold drafts or near air conditioners.

Fertilization

Fertilize monthly during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced, diluted liquid fertilizer (e.g., 10-10-10). This helps the plant maintain its healthy growth and vibrant foliage.

Pruning

Regular pruning is essential to maintain the plant’s shape and prevent it from becoming too leggy. Trim any long, spindly stems and remove damaged or yellowing leaves to encourage new growth and keep the plant healthy.

Humidity

Philodendrons prefer moderate to high humidity. The higher the humidity, the better they thrive, which makes them an excellent choice for bathrooms, kitchens, or areas with a humidifier.

Air Circulation

While philodendrons don’t need high airflow, good air circulation helps to prevent fungal infections and mold growth, particularly if the plant is kept in a humid room.

Pest Management

Philodendrons can be prone to pests like aphids, mealybugs, spider mites, and scale. These pests typically appear on the undersides of the leaves or on the stems.

Repotting

Repot your Philodendron every 1–2 years, or when the plant outgrows its pot. You’ll know it’s time to repot when you see roots growing through the drainage holes or when the plant appears root-bound.
