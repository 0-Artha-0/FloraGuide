String of Pearls (Senecio rowleyanus)

String of Pearls is a unique and eye-catching succulent known for its cascading strands of round, bead-like leaves. Native to the arid regions of Southwest Africa, it’s an ideal plant for hanging planters where its long strands can trail beautifully.

Fun fact: The bead-shaped leaves help this plant conserve water, making it especially drought-tolerant.

Watering

As a succulent, String of Pearls is sensitive to overwatering. It prefers a “soak and dry” method: water thoroughly, then allow the soil to dry out completely before the next watering.

Frequency

Water approximately once every 2–3 weeks, more often in warmer months and less during the winter when the plant’s growth slows. Always check that the soil is dry before watering.

Technique

Water the soil directly, avoiding the leaves to prevent rot. Water until it drains from the pot’s bottom, then empty any excess water from the saucer.

Soil

String of Pearls needs a well-draining, gritty soil. A cactus or succulent potting mix works best, or you can mix regular potting soil with sand or perlite for additional drainage.

Light

Bright, indirect light is ideal for this plant. It tolerates some direct sunlight, especially morning light, but too much direct sun can scorch the leaves. Place it near a south or west-facing window for optimal light.

Temperature

String of Pearls thrives in temperatures between 65°F and 80°F (18°C to 27°C). It can tolerate slightly cooler temperatures, but avoid frost, as it is sensitive to the cold.

Fertilization

Feed once a month during the growing season (spring and summer) with a diluted, balanced succulent fertilizer. Over-fertilizing can cause leggy growth, so apply sparingly..

Pruning

Prune occasionally to remove any dead or shriveled pearls and to maintain a tidy shape. You can also trim longer strands to encourage fuller growth at the base.

Humidity

As a succulent, String of Pearls prefers low to average humidity. It does well in typical indoor humidity levels and does not require additional humidity support.

Air Circulation

Good air circulation is beneficial, especially after watering, as it helps prevent the roots from staying too damp. However, avoid placing the plant directly in drafty spots.

Pest Management

String of Pearls is prone to pests like aphids, mealybugs, and spider mites. Inspect the plant regularly, and if pests appear, treat with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Repotting

Repot every 1–2 years in spring. Choose a shallow pot with drainage holes and refresh the soil to keep it loose and well-draining.
