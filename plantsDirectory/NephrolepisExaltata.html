Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)

The Boston Fern is a classic houseplant known for its feathery, arching fronds and lush appearance. It’s especially popular in hanging baskets, adding a touch of greenery to any indoor space.

Watering

Boston Ferns like their soil to stay consistently moist, but not soggy. Water when the top of the soil feels dry to the touch, but don’t allow it to dry out completely. These ferns prefer high humidity, so regular misting or placing them on a pebble tray can help. Be sure the pot has excellent drainage.

Frequency

Water 1-2 times per week, or as needed to keep the soil consistently moist Boston Ferns do not tolerate drought well.

Technique

Water thoroughly until water drains from the bottom, ensuring the soil stays evenly moist but not soggy.

Soil

Prefers moist, well-draining potting soil rich in organic matter. A peat-based mix works well for retaining moisture.

Light

Indirect sunlight or low light. Place it in a spot with filtered light or partial shade; direct sunlight can scorch its fronds.

Temperature

Ideal temperature range is 60-75°F (16-24°C). Protect from cold drafts, as Boston Ferns are sensitive to temperature fluctuations.

Fertilization

Lightly fertilize monthly during the growing season with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer.

Pruning

Trim off any yellow, brown, or damaged fronds to keep the plant looking lush. Regular pruning promotes fresh, healthy growth.

Humidity

High humidity is essential for Boston Ferns. Mist daily or place the plant near a humidifier, especially in dry environments.

Air Circulation

Low to moderate air circulation is ideal. Avoid placing the plant in a very windy spot or next to air conditioning vents.

Pest Management

Check regularly for spider mites and mealybugs. If pests appear, treat with insecticidal soap or use a damp cloth to wipe them off.

Repotting

Repot every 1-2 years in fresh soil. If the fern has outgrown its pot, move it to a slightly larger one in the spring.
