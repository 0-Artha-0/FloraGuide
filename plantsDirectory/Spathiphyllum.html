Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum)

The Peace Lily is a popular indoor plant known for its lush, dark green foliage and elegant white blooms. It's a resilient, easy-to-care-for plant that also purifies the air, making it an ideal choice for homes and offices.

Watering

Peace Lilies prefer consistently moist soil but can’t tolerate being waterlogged. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings, but avoid letting the plant sit in dry soil for too long. Overwatering can lead to root rot, so ensure the pot has proper drainage. It's best to water when the soil feels slightly dry to the touch, and make sure to empty any excess water from the saucer beneath the pot.

Frequency

Water about once a week, or when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. They are sensitive to overwatering, so always check before watering.

Technique

To water, pour water evenly across the soil until it drains out the bottom of the pot. Peace Lilies will wilt when they need water, giving a visual cue that it's time to hydrate.

Soil

Peace Lilies prefer well-draining, slightly acidic potting soil. A mix designed for houseplants or one with added perlite works well to ensure good drainage.

Light

They thrive in low to medium indirect light, making them suitable for rooms without a lot of sunlight. Avoid placing them in direct sunlight, which can scorch the leaves.

Temperature

Peace Lilies do best in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C), making typical room temperatures ideal.

Fertilization

Feed monthly during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced liquid fertilizer to encourage healthy growth.

Pruning

Remove any yellow, brown, or dead leaves at the base to keep the plant looking fresh. This also helps prevent disease.

Humidity

These plants enjoy high humidity. Mist them occasionally or place them near a humidifier to mimic their natural tropical environment.

Air Circulation

Peace Lilies benefit from moderate air circulation, so ensure the plant isn't in a completely closed-off space.

Pest Management

Watch for common pests such as aphids, mealybugs, and spider mites. If they appear, treat with insecticidal soap or wipe the leaves with a damp cloth.

Repotting

Repot every 1-2 years or when the plant becomes rootbound. Choose a pot only slightly larger to prevent waterlogging and soil saturation.
